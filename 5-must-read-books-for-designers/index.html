5 Must-read books for designers

  • Hugo
  • November 3, 2023
  • Blog, Design, Tips

If you are a designer or are interested in design start with these 5 must-read books for designers. In this article I will share with you some of my favorite design books and why I like them so much.

Photo by Ugur Akdemir on Unsplash

Hey everyone, lately Ive been rereading a few design books that I have around, mainly because of this article. Today I hope to share with you the 5 books that, in my opinion, should be on everyones shelves.

In a nutshell here are the books Ill be talking about:

  • The Vignelli Canon
  • Stop Stealing Sheep Find How Type Works
  • Thinking with Type
  • Logotype & Symbol
  • The Book Block and The Book Cover

As per usual I would love to see what your suggestions for essential books are! Eventually, I would love to write another article on this topic, so send me your suggestions and they may be featured next time.

The Vignelli Canon

Photo by David Hall

Written by the late iconic designer Massimo Vignelli, The Vignelli Canon is in my opinion of the most important design books ever made. It serves as a guide to the principles of what good design is while at the same time giving us incredible insights into Vignellis thought process, methodology, and philosophy.

From the iconic list of 6 typefaces, I talked about in my last article to the core ideas behind his designs, this book serves almost as a bible for anyone looking to become a designer.

In an age where we are seeing more and more minimalist work being produced for the sake of being minimalist or style trends bringing back brutalism and maximalism, The Vignelli Canon is a nice reminder of what design isnt and what it should be.

I truly believe everyone would love to read the lessons in this book, it is available to be bought here or totally free to download here. Amazingly, this book is completely free being yet another small detail that gives us a glimpse of the type of individual Vignelli was.

Doing the research for this article I also came across this episode of the podcast Design Matters with Debbie Milman, although it was released 10 years ago, this episode still holds up and personally, I believe you will find a lot of value in it.

Stop Stealing Sheep Find How Type Works

Photo by Erik Spiekermann

As a total fanboy, I really like this book written by also legendary designer and typographer Erik Spiekermann! “Stop Stealing Sheep Find How Type Works is an incredible journey that leaves us understanding how important type is and how to use it.

This 4th edition is updated with a new chapter on variable typefaces, several examples on how to effectively communicate with type, and a full selection of new typefaces used and referenced throughout the book.

Although its a compact book it encourages reading and I guarantee every time you open it up you will find and learn something new.

This book is available to be bought through Eriks experimental type gallery P98A and its also available for free on Google Fonts, which is awesome!

Thinking with type

Photo by Konstantinas Ladauskas

Do I have to say anything? Ellen Luptons iconic book Thinking with Type was released a long time ago but it still holds up as it is frequently updated by the author. Lets be real your university probably had this book in their references and for a good reason!

Thinking with Type is a comprehensive guide to topics such as font selection, spacing, grids, and layout design, providing practical advice and examples for designers.

I frequently find myself rereading this book and I learn something new every time! You can acquire a copy here. Unfortunately, there isnt a free copy of this book I can link to you, perhaps one day Google Fonts will collaborate with Ellen, that would be awesome!

Logotype & Symbol

Photo by Inkbot Design

If youre like me and many other designers in the world seeking inspiration and references is basically a part of our day-to-day routines. When we talk about branding projects having good references for inspiration that you can pull out at any time is also essential.

While for many years the holy grail of logo reference has been a book called Logo Modernism, which is a mammoth of a book, today I want to tell you about the two alternatives Logotype & Symbol.

Like Logo Modernism these two books, which I personally think you should buy together, serve as a more digestible alternative. If you already own the mammoth you will find Logotype & Symbol still useful as there isnt much duplicated content between books.

Personally, I really like these and whenever Im working on any kind of branding project, they are extremely useful, if you are in a team their size is also useful to share between people.

You can buy a copy here, which allows you to choose between a physical or a digital copy which is also cool!

The Book Block and The Book Cover

Photo by Lus Espinheira

Last but not least a suggestion that Im sure you will love. The Book Block and The Book Cover are perfect books for anyone looking to work on editorial design, they are incredibly useful tools.

Created by Portuguese studio itemzero they present, in a very straight to the point useful way the key ingredients needed to design a great publication.

Explaining different techniques with a plethora of variations this book is in my view essential for anyone working in the industry.

Last but not least for my Portuguese audience it also is multilingual which is awesome. You can acquire a copy here, they frequently have discounts so stay alert!

On a final note, I hope you found these books useful and please remember to share your must-haves

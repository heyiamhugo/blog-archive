5 important things bloggers need to know

  • Hugo
  • November 21, 2023
  • Blog, Tips

After about a month in the blogging scene I’ve learned a few important things that leveled up my content, today I want to share with you these 5 things that you and all bloggers need to know!

Photo by Daniel Thomas on Unsplash

Around a month ago I was in my bedroom thinking about writing and how I could improve my lackluster skills, suddenly an idea came to mind – what if I start a blog? I could write about anything that I felt truly passionate about and in return, I would improve my skills. Pretty good deal.

After this first month of writing about several topics that varied from tips to my latest review of the MX Keys S Combo, I’ve been having a lot of fun. Most of my readers have been friends and random online strangers which I’m very grateful for! Seriously reading the few comments I got and seeing people interact with whatever I posted was genuinely awesome and I hope it just continues to grow onwards.

Although I want to be honest with you, I write not because I’m looking for approval or adoring fans but rather as a therapy for my creative mind – seriously.

Nowadays mindless content being pushed into our dopamine-filled heads is almost a day-to-day guarantee, so being able to slow down and write about whatever goes on in my mind is awesome.

But anyway, if you haven’t read my very first article, I would recommend it as I go into why I started blogging and how you can also start your own thing.

Today’s article is in many ways a follow up, what are the lessons that I learnt from my first month blogging? Well in this article I’m going to share with you, fellow reader, 5 important things bloggers need to know.

1 – Besides you, no one cares

I previously mentioned the impact of the current state of content and how blogging could be a refuge in this modern-day dopamine apocalypse, the truth is that fewer people are going to read whatever you write – consequences.

To me, this is fine, I wasn’t expecting my blog to become the next The New Yorker or to compete for the attention of people, but I very quickly realized that it was going to become truly, incredibly difficult to reach people.

To add to the pressure point, I feel like most people who are even reading blogs are looking for solutions to problems, they want to quickly get an answer.

In consequence, more thought-based articles like this one fall into oblivion a lot of the times, which I think is rather interesting.

As I previously said, to me this isn’t a problem, I write because I wanted a place where I could improve my writing skills and share some thoughts. I was not expecting a huge audience, and you shouldn’t as well!

Write for the sake of writing, because it is a fun thing to do, not because it will be a foolish obligatory thing adding up to the 50 other foolish obligatory things you’re doing.

If you are the only one who cares, accept and keep writing when you feel like it. I said I would write about 1-3 articles per week, I’ve managed to consistently write 2 articles every week, last week I was not feeling it and I simply didn’t write a second article. You must not feel obligated to do something that should be fun to do, I was not in the mood I simply did not wrote an article – period.

2 – Get to the point

People want solutions to problems they don’t have, they don’t have time, they don’t care about the person writing. Creating a piece of content that people will at least read means you have to get to the point. Even if it means writing shorter articles.

People usually like a well-paced story over a boring 2000-page eternal journey that never ends. So, give them that.

But also write fun stuff, stuff that you would want to read and share with others. Keep it smart and simple.

Getting to the point is hard but don’t forget design school – less is more. Don’t go around 1000 words to say you really hate oranges, just say you hate oranges and explain why you hate oranges after that. People will be much more invested.

3 – Pose questions and solve problems

To me most articles I write either come from a question that I have, a problem that I wish was solved, or just an urge to write about something.

Out of all the posts I wrote the past month the ones I wrote that posed questions and offered solutions were the ones to get the most amount of engagement.

So, the next time you write an article for your blog think about this, what problem do you have a solution for? What question do you have that others may also have?

Humans are inherently curious beings, so like the little kid you once were, ask questions, lots of them.

4 – Be human

In an age where most content you now see online is basically generated by AI, being human will in my view be very important in the future.

Showing my human side is something that I never imagined I would struggle with, but I have come up with my own conclusions about that.

Write in the first person, address the audience, be direct, share experiences, and don’t be afraid of being silly.

Throughout my articles, I really tried to employ these tactics in order for my blog to be or feel more human to whoever reads it. By talking about my dad on a post, my struggles as a student, or just simply by sharing a Spider-Man meme, suddenly what was once a robotic post now feels more human, more relatable, more me, more you.

5 – What do you want to read?

Although writing for the sake of writing is a good thing, no judging. Writing about something that you are interested in inherently allows you to understand people in your target audience.

The next time you write an article don’t just think about what you want to write, think about what you would also like to read.

Writing, like many things, is a constant exercise that takes time, takes practice and allows for development. My first articles probably weren’t that good, but they served a purpose – to allow me to grow.

If you take this into consideration, you will feel much more comfortable, writing will not be so much about an audience, but it will be more like this thing we do for us that others can be a part of.  

If you write what you want to read it will also provide you with more ideas, make you more invested and consequently make you create better articles.

So, the next time you write, think about those who read!

As part of the design community, I see this space as a place of sharing, so if you are also starting a blog or have your blog share it below, it could be a blog about anything, I’m interested to read what you write about in your free time.
